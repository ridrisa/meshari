Solar-Powered Electric Vehicle Charging Stations

Business Case for Eco-System Future Challenge

By: Meshari AlMoqbel

Literature Review

Saudi Arabia's significant reliance on petroleum and gas has resulted in high carbon emissions, impacting climate change and weather, increasing temperatures, and threatening environmental health. With initiatives like the Kingdom Vision 2030, there is a global movement toward clean energy.

Needs Statement Points

  • Building EV Infrastructure: Encourages electric vehicle ownership through charging infrastructure.
  • Reducing Carbon Emissions: Uses solar energy to lower carbon emissions.
  • Promoting Sustainable Energy: Aligns with the Saudi Arabian 2030 vision for alternative energy.

Direct Key Benefits

  • Impact: Decrease carbon emissions and establish an eco-friendly station culture.
  • Partnerships: Collaborations with companies like Lucid and Ceer.
  • Tangible Benefits: High market share potential until competitors arise.
  • Environmental Benefits: Promote clean energy, reducing reliance on gasoline.
  • Regional Leadership: Regional leadership in renewable energy with extensive support.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

First in the Market, government support, aligned with Vision 2030, lower operational and maintenance costs.

Weaknesses

High technology complexity, public awareness, and acceptance.

Opportunities

Full market share, enables new business models, leads regional renewable energy initiatives.

Threats

High setup costs, licensing, and weather challenges.

Project Structured Plan

  1. Project Information: Solar-Powered EV Charging Stations, from 1/1/2025 to 30/06/2025, with a budget of $200,000.
  2. Collecting Requirements: Data gathering and SME consultation.
  3. Key Deliverables: Deliver the first solar charging station for EVs.
